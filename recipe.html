Deviled Eggs

How to make deviled and eggs and rule

Deviled eggs is a simple dish that will instantly make you the favorite in the potluck dinner scene. Forget _____ salads and crispy things with dip, deviled eggs are the talk of the table with the, "I haven't had this in years!" Easy to make and perfect for finger eating, it's always a win win with these devils.

Ingredients

  • 12 large eggs
  • 3 tbsp. mayonnaise
  • 3 tsp. mustard
  • Water!
  • Paprika
  • Chives or other fresh herbs for garnishing

    • Tools from the kitchen

  • Saucepan for boiling
  • Mixing bowl
  • A proper knife
  • Spoon
  • Ziploc bag (optional if you're resourceful)

Boiling eggs is a lost art

  1. Place enough water in the saucepan so that the eggs will be fully immersed while boiling. Bring the water to a boil.
  2. Once water is boiling, reduce heat to a simmer and place the eggs into the saucepan. Cover and let the eggs heat for 15-20 mins.
  3. When eggs are sufficiently heated, drain the water, remove the eggs and place them in a bowl with cold water and ice. Eggs can then either be placed in the fridge or freezer - not too long! - to cool them down before peeling.
  4. Gently crack the eggs and peel them. Give them a rinse to ensure all shell and peels are removed.

Mixing time

  1. Cut the eggs from pole to pole, remove the yolk and place yolks in mixing bowl. Set empty white aside.
  2. Add the mayonnaise and mustard to the mixing bowl.
  3. With a spoon, mix the ingredients in the bowl until smooth.
  4. The mash can either be placed into a Ziploc bag with a corner cut to squeeze back on top of the egg whites. Or, just use a spoon and be careful.
  5. Once the mash is all finished, dash some paprika, chives or fresh herbs of your choice on top of the eggs.
  6. Place in a bowl or plate, cover and let sit in the fridge for an hour or so to allow eggs to get cold.

Time to eat!
